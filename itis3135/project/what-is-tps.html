What is a Treatment Planning System?

A Treatment Planning System (TPS) is a critical tool used in radiation therapy for cancer treatment. It allows oncologists and medical physicists to plan how radiation will be delivered to a patient's tumor while minimizing exposure to surrounding healthy tissue.

Key Functions of a TPS

  • Importing and visualizing CT/MRI scan data
  • Defining target volumes (tumor) and organs at risk (OARs)
  • Calculating dose distributions using physics-based models
  • Visualizing dose-volume histograms (DVH)
  • Exporting the final treatment plan to clinical systems
