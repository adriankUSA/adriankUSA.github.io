What is a Treatment Planning System (TPS)?

A Treatment Planning System is a software application and tool used in radiation therapy to help plan, simulate, and evaluate the best course of radiation for cancer patients.

What does a TPS do?

A TPS performs several critical functions:

  • Dose Calculation: Estimates how much radiation reaches tissues.
  • Imaging Integration: Uses CT/MRI scans for 3D planning.
  • Contouring & ROI: Defines tumor and healthy tissue boundaries.
  • Treatment Simulation: Visualizes and simulates treatment scenarios.
What is Dose Calculation?

Treatment planning systems are able to analyze special regions of interests, such as tumors gathered from DICOM data, and calculates radiation beam dosage for the area, while also highlighting the area in which radiation should be applied.

What is Imaging Integration?

A TPS parses medical imaging data (DICOM files, CT, MRI) and maps the internal anatomy of the patient and align radiation targets accurately.

What is Contouring & ROI?

Clinicians outline regions of interest (ROIs), such as tumors and critical structures, to guide treatment focus and dose distribution.

What is Treatment Simulation?

TPS software simulates radiation delivery so clinicians can visualize the effects and fine-tune settings before actual treatment.
